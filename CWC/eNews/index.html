755 East Porterville Homes Now Connected to Safe and Reliable Water Source!

Breaking ground on the first connection in the East Porterville Water Supply Project in 2016.

Residents of East Porterville knew first-hand the impacts of California’s severe drought better than most, with hundreds of private wells becoming dry during that time. Some families went over three years without reliable, running water in their homes. Due to the advocacy of community residents, including the community based organization East Porterville for Water Justice, officials at the state and local level worked together to bring a long-term drinking water solution to the community of East Porterville by connecting homes to the nearby City of Porterville water system. See more on the project in Department of Water Resources’ video here.

Community Water Center helped this effort by supporting the organizing efforts of East Porterville for Water Justice and conducting large-scale outreach to community to ensure residents knew what their options were and what they could expect if they chose to connect to the City of Porterville water system. We are now so pleased to say that 755 homes have a source of safe and reliable water for years to come!
